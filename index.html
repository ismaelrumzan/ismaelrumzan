About

Ismael has been focusing on Educational Technology since 2001 after completing his Post Doctoral research in the Geophysics Department, University of Alberta. Since then, he has been in the field of training and technology development. He was co-founder for LGL Systems (USA) that powered online educational institutes and supported 4000 students yearly.

He has worked or freelanced for organizations such as University of Alberta, Greater than Technology Corporation, Razi Education, Qasid Institute and Al Jazeera. He is passionate about using JavaScript and its frameworks to reproduce the work of the user experience designer and create ubiquitous user experiences.

Live Virtual Classroom Tool

2006-2009: LGL SYSTEMS VIRTUAL CLASSROOM COMMUNICATION SOFTWARE WITH RECORDINGS

Instructors needed to have a rich communication experience with students based on the traditional way of religious teaching. We developed Flash-based multimedia virtual classroom software of the class of Adobe Connect to enable this rich communication. Furthermore, recordings that reproduced the live experience as close as possible was vital as many students could not attend live. Both of these software have gone through multiple revisions and improvements based on usability tests and student interviews. It has become one of the key enablers of success for an online academy that grew from 200-300 to 800-1000 students per semester.

Role: Instructional Designer, Interaction Designer, Flex/Flash/Flash Media Server Specialist, Technology lead

HKN Tutorials

2003: A self-paced instructional resource to learn how to search for medical and health information effectively

This resource was organized in 3 levels with each level presenting real scenarios that healthcare professionals need to perform. Once the objectives were clearly presented, they move to a 2-frame based navigation where on the left they have specific instructions on the tasks and on the right, they can log in the database to perform the tasks. These were enhanced, specially for visual learners, with images of interface elements and “Demos” for complex tasks. Test yourself interactive simulations of specific tasks were also available for students to check their knowledge.

Role: Instructional Technologist, Flash animator, Web specialist
